Roman Legionaries

Roman legionaries were the professional soldiers who made up the Roman legions. They were famous for their discipline and organization. Each legion had about 5,000 soldiers.

A legionary would sign up for 25 years of service! They built roads, bridges, and forts as well as fighting in battles. The Roman army conquered lands from Britain to Egypt, making Rome into a mighty empire.

Roman Senators

Roman senators were the lawmakers in ancient Rome. The Senate began during the Roman Republic around 509 BC and continued through the Empire period.

Only wealthy men could be senators, and they wore special togas with purple stripes to show their importance. The Senate met in a building called the Curia in the Roman Forum to debate laws and make decisions for Rome.

Roman Gladiators

Gladiators were fighters who battled in arenas for public entertainment. Many gladiators were slaves or prisoners of war who were forced to fight, though some free men chose to become gladiators for fame and rewards.

Different types of gladiators had different weapons and armor. For example, the "retiarius" fought with a net and trident, while the "secutor" had a helmet, shield and sword. The Colosseum in Rome was the most famous place for gladiator fights.

Roman Emperors

Roman emperors were the absolute rulers of the Roman Empire. The first emperor was Augustus, who came to power in 27 BC after the end of the Roman Republic.

Emperors wore purple clothing (the most expensive dye) because only they were allowed to wear all purple. They also wore gold laurel wreaths or crowns. Some famous emperors include Trajan, Hadrian, Marcus Aurelius, and Constantine who made Christianity legal in Rome.
